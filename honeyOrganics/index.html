The Production Cycle

  • Collect Nectar: Worker bees visit flowers to collect nectar.
  • Store Nectar: The nectar is stored in a special stomach called the honey stomach.
  • Pass the Nectar: Back at the hive, the worker bees pass the nectar to younger "house bees" through a process called trophallaxis(passing mouth-to-mouth).
  • Enzymatic Breakdown: The house bees chew the nectar adding an enzyme called invertase. This breaks down the complex sugar into simpler sugar, giving honey its sweetness.
  • storing: The bees spread the nectar in honeycomb cells
  • Dehydration: Bees fan their wings to evaporate excess water from the nectar.
  • Sealing the Honey: Once the honey reaches the desired consistency, the bees cap the honeycomb cells with wax for the preservation
after that bees started to consume honey according to their need and this helps them to survive during winters when flower nectar become scarce.
